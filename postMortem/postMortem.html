POST MORTEM DIGITUS [2023]

speculative archive

Scanned Photoalbums, saved screenshots, childhood recordings and many more relics saved digitally are assumed to last forever - or at least longer than its physical counterpart. However, ordinary Harddrives have an approximate 3 - 5 years lifespan. The cloud is promising us endless storage and time, but it too is constructed out of servers and hardware: We demand immortality from these machines, but have we ever found a way to truly preserve fragile memory?

Post Mortem Digitus proposes a speculative future, in which the death of a Harddrive is examined, determined and dissected: By investigating the dead we can understand the living, by taking a look at the mortal remains the memory seems immortal.
